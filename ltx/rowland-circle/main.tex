\subsection{Ray tracing simulations}

\textbf{You will:}

\begin{itemize}
    \item understand the effect of crystal radius in energy resolution and focusing conditions
    \item calculate the focusing conditions in and out Rowland configuration 
    \item understand the importance of using contour curves with PlotXY simulate an asymmetric crystal
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Questions:}

\textbf{i)} Using a Gaussian source ($\sigma_x$=10$\mu$m, $\sigma_z$=10$\mu$m, $\sigma_x'$=10$\mu$rad, $\sigma_z'$=10$\mu$rad), calculate the radius of curvature for the focusing conditions $p=q=$30~m for a symmetrical Si111 Bragg crystal at 10 keV. Calculate $Delta E$. This was the Rowland condition.
Calculate now $Delta E$ for $R$=50 m and $R$=25 m (both off-Rowland). Explain the differences. 

\textbf{ii)} Calculate the Rowland conditions for 10 keV, Si111, p=30m and asymmetry angle $\alpha$=5 deg. Calculate energy resolution and spot size. 

\textbf{Hints:} use {\tt rowland.ows}


\textbf{Answer}

i)  The radius of curvature from the Coddington equations is $R$=151.733~m (calculated by the script). 
It is implemented the first branch, giving $\Delta E$=1.32 eV and focus at 30 m (verified using the caustic widget) of 1.32 $\mu$m.



For $R$=50~m (second branch) we then have $\Delta E$=2.6 eV and the best focus is found using the caustic widget at $q$=5.95~m and where size is 5.5 $\mu$m. 

For $R$=25~m (third branch) we then have $\Delta E \gt $= 5 eV and the best focus is found using the caustic widget at $q$=2.7~m and where size is 2.24 $\mu$m. 

ii) Using the script to calculate Rowland conditions, we get $R$=10.623 mm, $q$=11.848 m; We get  $Delta E$=0.78 eV; spot size of 170 $\mu$m \textcolor{red}{found at $q\approx$6~m}.
